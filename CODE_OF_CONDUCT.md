# 🤝 Code of Conduct

## 🌱 Our Commitment

We are committed to fostering an open, welcoming, respectful, and inclusive
community.

As contributors, maintainers, and community members, we pledge to make
participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone,
regardless of:

- age
- body size
- disability
- ethnicity
- gender identity or expression
- level of experience
- nationality
- personal appearance
- race
- religion
- sexual identity or orientation
- socio-economic status
- technical background

We aim to build systems, tools, and communities that encourage:

- collaboration
- curiosity
- empathy
- healthy communication
- sustainable development practices

---

## 🧭 Community Standards

## ✅ Examples of Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contribute to a positive environment include:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward others
- Respecting differing perspectives and experiences
- Giving and accepting constructive feedback gracefully
- Prioritizing collaboration over ego
- Taking responsibility for mistakes and learning from them
- Supporting healthy and sustainable engineering practices
- Helping create psychologically safe environments for learning and contribution
- Focusing on long-term maintainability and shared understanding

---

### ❌ Examples of Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, discrimination, or hateful conduct
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments
- Personal attacks or intimidation
- Publishing private information without explicit permission
- Sustained disruptive behavior
- Sexualized language or unwanted advances
- Deliberately misleading contributors or maintainers
- Bad-faith collaboration or intentionally hostile review behavior
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

---

## 🏛️ Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing community
standards fairly and consistently.

Maintainers may:

- remove or edit comments, commits, issues, discussions, or pull requests
- temporarily or permanently restrict participation
- request behavioral changes before further participation

Enforcement decisions will prioritize:

- community safety
- fairness
- transparency where appropriate
- long-term project health

---

## 🌐 Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including:

- GitHub repositories

- pull requests
- issue trackers

## ⚖️ Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders may apply the following consequences depending on severity and
context.

### 1. Correction

**Impact:** Minor inappropriate behavior or unprofessional conduct.

**Response:** Private clarification and guidance regarding expected behavior.

---

### 2. Warning

**Impact:** Repeated or more serious violations.

**Response:** Formal warning with clear expectations for improvement and notice
of consequences for continued behavior.

---

### 3. Temporary Restriction

**Impact:** Serious or sustained violations of community standards.

**Response:** Temporary suspension from project participation or communication
channels.

---

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Impact:** Severe harassment, threats, discrimination, abuse, or repeated
violations.

**Response:** Permanent removal from all project spaces and participation.

---

## 🤖 AI-Assisted Contributions

AI-assisted contributions are welcome, but contributors remain responsible for:

- validating generated content
- ensuring correctness and maintainability
- reviewing security implications
- following project conventions and standards

AI-generated contributions should enhance collaboration and productivity — not
replace accountability or thoughtful engineering practices.
