# 📜 Governance

## Purpose

This document describes how decisions are made, how maintainers collaborate, and how the project evolves over time.

The goal is to keep the project open, transparent, and sustainable while avoiding unnecessary bureaucracy.

---

## Governance Philosophy

This project follows a pragmatic governance model:

* Transparency over secrecy.
* Discussion over conflict.
* Consensus over voting whenever possible.
* Simplicity over process.

Contributors are encouraged to participate in discussions, propose improvements, and help shape the future direction of the project.

---

## Roles

### Contributors

Anyone who participates in the project.

Examples include:

* Reporting issues.
* Improving documentation.
* Submitting pull requests.
* Participating in discussions.
* Providing feedback and testing.

---

### Maintainers

Maintainers are responsible for the day-to-day stewardship of the project.

Responsibilities include:

* Reviewing contributions.
* Managing releases.
* Maintaining project quality.
* Guiding technical direction.
* Ensuring a welcoming community environment.

Maintainers may approve, request changes to, or decline contributions based on the long-term goals of the project.

---

### Project Lead

The Project Lead is ultimately responsible for the project's direction and governance.

Responsibilities include:

* Resolving unresolved disputes.
* Making final decisions when consensus cannot be reached.
* Appointing maintainers.
* Managing governance updates.

Projects may have one or more Project Leads.

---

## Decision Making

Most decisions should be made through discussion and consensus.

Examples include:

* Documentation updates.
* Bug fixes.
* Refactoring.
* Routine maintenance.

For significant changes, maintainers may request broader discussion before proceeding.

Examples include:

* Major architectural changes.
* Breaking changes.
* Governance updates.
* Significant roadmap changes.

When consensus cannot be reached, the Project Lead may make a final decision.

---

## Contributions

Contributions of all sizes are welcome.

Contributors should follow:

* CONTRIBUTING.md
* CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md
* Project documentation and conventions

Acceptance of contributions is determined by maintainers based on project goals, quality standards, and long-term maintainability.

---

## Maintainer Appointments

Maintainers are appointed by the Project Lead.

Factors considered include:

* Quality of contributions.
* Consistency of participation.
* Technical understanding.
* Community engagement.
* Alignment with project values.

There is no fixed contribution threshold.

Appointments are based on trust, judgment, and demonstrated stewardship.

---

## Code of Conduct

All participants are expected to follow the project's Code of Conduct.

Violations may result in:

* Warnings.
* Temporary restrictions.
* Removal of contributor privileges.
* Permanent bans in severe cases.

Enforcement decisions are made by maintainers and, when necessary, the Project Lead.

---

## Governance Changes

This document may evolve as the project grows.

Governance changes should be discussed openly whenever practical.

The Project Lead retains final authority over governance updates.
